Face Recognition and Automatic Sign-In System for Complex Classroom Scenes

Nov. 2023 – Nov. 2024
Research Member · Advisor: Prof. Kang An

Collaboratively built a system that integrates trained deep learning models, including YOLOv8 and FaceNet, for face recognition and person counting in challenging classroom scenes. The system included a variable-zoom pan-tilt camera, an automatic sign-in program, and a management app.

  • Improved the network structures of YOLOv8 and FaceNet by sharing convolutional layers, adding attention mechanisms, and using feature fusion to support simultaneous face recognition and people counting.
  • Collected classroom images under occlusion, side-angle faces, low resolution, uneven lighting, and misaligned illumination conditions.
  • Addressed facial recognition challenges by making the algorithm more sensitive to pixel-value edges and texture information.
  • Programmed the automatic sign-in program connected to a live camera and a Bluetooth-linked management app.
  • Designed the mechanical structure of the variable-focus pan-tilt unit to reduce blind spots in different classroom layouts.
YOLOv8 FaceNet Face Recognition Person Counting Bluetooth App

YOLOv5 Algorithm-Based Traffic Light Recognition Guide Cane with Voice Reminder

Jun. 2023 – Aug. 2024
Lead Researcher · Funded Project of Shanghai College Students Innovation & Entrepreneurial Training Program · Advisors: Prof. Bo Zhang and Prof. Xiao Lin

Led the development of a guide-cane prototype that uses YOLOv5-based traffic-light recognition and a voice reminder module to provide real-time assistance in outdoor scenarios.

  • Trained a YOLOv5 model on traffic-light images collected under various real-world conditions.
  • Tuned model parameters and refined training strategies to improve recognition stability and reliability.
  • Performed extensive field tests and continuously improved the model based on real-world performance.
  • Used Google APIs and gTTS to implement a voice interaction module for real-time spoken feedback.
  • Created mechanical drawings for the cane and integrated the machine vision and voice modules into the physical prototype.
YOLOv5 Computer Vision gTTS Assistive Device Prototype Design

Optimizing Path Planning and Conflict Resolution in High-Density AS/RS with a Multi-Shuttle Cooperative Scheduling Framework

Jul. 2023 – May 2024
Research Member · Principal Investigator: Prof. Kang An

Contributed to a project that proposed a grid-map-based Multi-Shuttle Cooperative Scheduling Model to address individual shuttle path planning and multi-shuttle collision avoidance in high-density automated storage and retrieval systems.

  • Assisted in developing a Fuzzy A* path-planning algorithm that considers turning cost and obstacle distribution to balance efficiency and path length.
  • Helped design a grid-map-based adaptive time-window algorithm to improve shuttle and track utilization.
  • Supported validation experiments in both real and simulated warehouse environments.
  • Contributed to the research work that was later published in IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement.
Path Planning Fuzzy A* Collision Avoidance Cooperative Scheduling AS/RS

An OpenMV-Based Autonomous Navigation and Pesticide Fixed-Point Spray Robot

Nov. 2022 – Nov. 2023
Lead Researcher · Advisor: Prof. Ye Yang

Led a team to develop a robot capable of autonomous navigation, movement control, visual recognition of plant damage, and fixed-point pesticide spraying with controlled spray volume and timing.

  • Designed and assembled the overall robot, including its mechanical structure, modules, and working mechanisms.
  • Coordinated team members to complete mechanical-structure sketches and subsystem designs.
  • Programmed, adjusted, and implemented the robot’s autonomous navigation, movement, and target-recognition modules.
  • Participated in testing, debugging, and adjusting code for other robot modules.
  • Researched and selected appropriate accessories for the robot prototype.

Achievement: The prototype won Second Prize in the 2023 National Undergraduate Mechanical Innovation Competition and led to a utility model patent.
